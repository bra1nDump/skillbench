## Ranking Rationale

### #1 — Harvey AI (NEW)

**Why #1:** Harvey is the undisputed revenue leader in legal AI — the largest sub-category in business tools. $190M+ ARR (3.9x YoY), $8–11B valuation, 100K lawyers, 98% gross revenue retention. Eight of the ten highest-grossing US law firms are customers. No other business tool in this ranking has comparable revenue traction.

**Evidence basis:**
- [STRONG] $190M+ ARR confirmed by Artificial Lawyer (independent trade press) and Sacra (independent analyst)
- [STRONG] $1.2B+ total raised across 7 rounds; $11B valuation talks (Sequoia + GIC, Feb 2026)
- [STRONG] 1,000 clients across 60 countries, 100K lawyers
- [STRONG] 98% gross revenue retention; median seat count doubles within 12 months
- [STRONG] Multi-model orchestration (GPT-4, Claude, Gemini), custom-agent builder, "Memory" personalization

**Key risk:** Aggressive sales tactics and pricing backlash documented by multiple independent sources (Legally Disrupted, Reddit, The Lawyer). ~$1,200/lawyer/month with rigid 12-month contracts. Zero HN/developer community presence. Entirely closed, enterprise-only.

**Narrow subcase:** Harvey is best for BigLaw and large enterprise legal departments doing research, memo drafting, and multi-jurisdiction regulatory work. For Word-native contract drafting at small/mid-market firms, Spellbook wins.

### #2 — Ironclad (NEW)

**Why #2:** Highest-revenue CLM platform at $200M+ ARR. Gartner Magic Quadrant Leader for 3 consecutive years. First major CLM to ship autonomous contract agents (Mar 19, 2026). Customers include OpenAI, Salesforce, L'Oreal, Rivian, AMD.

**Evidence basis:**
- [STRONG] $200M+ ARR announced Feb 12, 2026 (PR Newswire, independently covered by Legal IT Insider)
- [STRONG] AI product Jurist delivered 6x ARR YoY growth; 65%+ of all customers adopted AI
- [STRONG] 4 AI Agents shipping (Renewal, Cost Savings, Archive, Intake); Intake Agent showed 50% reduction in submission time
- [STRONG] Gartner MQ Leader 2023, 2024, 2025

**Key risk:** New CEO Dan Springer (Apr 2025) — massive executive turnover signals either aggressive scaling or instability. Near-zero HN/developer presence.

**Narrow subcase:** Ironclad is best for enterprise legal ops managing the full contract lifecycle (pre-signature workflow, collaboration, approval). For post-signature analytics and contract intelligence on existing portfolios, LinkSquares is more focused.

## What Would Change This Ranking

1. **Harvey discloses churn or loses major client:** If aggressive sales tactics lead to visible departures from Am Law 100 firms, Spellbook or Ironclad could overtake.

2. **LEGALFLY Series B (April 2026):** A strong round ($30M+) with US expansion would push LEGALFLY up 2–3 ranks. A failed raise would freeze growth.

3. **LinkSquares permanent CEO appointment:** A strong permanent CEO would stabilize the narrative. Continued interim leadership would accelerate downgrade.

4. **Ironclad "Acting" launch (April 15, 2026):** If the autonomous agent-triggers-agent capability delivers, Ironclad could challenge Harvey for #1 in the broader business category.

5. **Luminance discloses ARR:** If Luminance reveals $80M+ ARR (implied by 2x growth for 2 years), it would jump to #3–4. If materially lower, it stays at #5.

6. **Gamma PPT export fix:** If Gamma fixes the most-cited complaint (layout breaks on PPT export), it removes Beautiful.ai's strongest differentiator.

7. **Spellbook acquisition execution:** If Spellbook successfully acquires 2–3 companies by Q4 2026, it could become the legal AI platform. Failed integrations would be damaging.

8. **MCP business server breakout:** No business MCP tool has >50 installs today. If one crosses 1K installs, it would enter the ranking immediately. Watch: SpotDraft MCP, PayPal MCP, GoHighLevel MCP.

9. **Canva ships AI-first presentation mode:** If Canva builds a Gamma-competitive AI presentation generator (not just template filler), it would reshape the sub-category overnight.

10. **QuickBooks AI agents go GA with premium pricing:** Would pressure Midday and all standalone invoicing tools in the freelancer/SMB segment.
