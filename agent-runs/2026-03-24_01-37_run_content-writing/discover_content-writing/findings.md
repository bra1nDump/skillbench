## Findings

### 1. META SHIFT: GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) Is the New Battleground

**The single biggest structural change since our last catalog update.** GEO — optimizing content to be cited by AI search engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini AI Overviews, Claude) — has become a critical capability. AI-referred sessions jumped **527% YoY** in the first 5 months of 2025 (Previsible report). By early 2026, most enterprise marketing teams have a GEO initiative.

**Who's moving:**
- **Frase** has rebranded as "The Agentic SEO & GEO Platform" — AI Visibility tracks your brand across 8 AI platforms (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Copilot, Grok, DeepSeek). Claims to be the only platform with read-write MCP access for GEO pipeline. Price dropped to **$15/mo** entry (was $38).
  - Source: https://www.frase.io/blog/what-is-generative-engine-optimization-geo
  - Why this matters: Frase is pulling ahead of Surfer on value AND adding a GEO moat. Catalog verdict needs updating.

- **Writesonic** has pivoted entirely to a "GEO platform" — tracks 15+ AI platforms, GEO optimization features. But core GEO features locked behind **$199-499/mo** plans.
  - Source: https://writesonic.com/ and https://www.rankability.com/blog/writesonic-geo-review/
  - Why this matters: Writesonic is betting the company on GEO but pricing itself out of SMB market. High-risk pivot.

- **Surfer SEO** added "AI Tracker" for monitoring how AI models reference your content.
  - Source: Web search results from multiple reviews
  - Why this matters: All three SEO content tools are adding GEO. First-mover advantage matters here.

### 2. Jasper AI Is in Serious Trouble

- Revenue dropped from **$120M ARR (2023 peak) → $35M (2024)** — a **53% decline**
- Founders stepped down September 2023; new CEO Timothy Young (ex-Dropbox president) installed
- Internal valuation cut by 20%
- Traffic down to ~4.2M visits/month (declining trend)
- Brand Voice remains the only defensible feature
- Multiple "Jasper alternatives" articles now dominate SEO for "Jasper AI"
- Source: https://www.maginative.com/article/jasper-cuts-internal-valuation-as-ai-growth-slows/
- Source: https://www.junia.ai/blog/jasper-ai-is-not-doing-well-whos-next-rytr
- **Why this matters:** Jasper's current catalog position needs a significant downward revision. The "Brand Voice" moat is real but the company may not survive long enough to exploit it. Claude Pro ($20/mo) and ChatGPT Plus ($20/mo) are eating the general content generation use case at 1/3 the price.

### 3. Copy.ai Has Pivoted to GTM AI Platform — No Longer Just a Writing Tool

- Rebranded as "GTM AI Platform" — workflows, agents, CRM integration
- Expanded agents: Prospecting Cockpit, Inbound Lead Processing, Account-Based Marketing, Translation + Localization, Deal Coaching
- No longer competes primarily on writing quality — competes on workflow automation
- Source: https://www.copy.ai/ and https://www.eesel.ai/blog/copy-ai
- **Why this matters:** Copy.ai's catalog entry frames it as a writing tool. It's now a marketing automation platform that happens to generate content. Category placement may need rethinking (content-writing + marketing + automation).

### 4. General-Purpose LLMs Are Actively Displacing Specialized Tools

Strong signal from multiple independent reviews and user experience posts:
- Claude "produces more readable, less AI-sounding content" than Jasper in blind tests — scores 85% on structure vs ChatGPT's 78%
- "For most writers, Claude Pro or ChatGPT Plus beats specialized tools at 1/3 the price" ($20/mo vs $49-69/mo)
- "A lot of people who switch end up using both: Claude for deep work, ChatGPT for quick creative tasks"
- Gemini Advanced's "Deep Research" can autonomously research + produce structured content with inline citations
- Source: https://www.zemith.com/en/contents/best-ai-for-writing-2026
- Source: https://genzwrite.com/blog/chatgpt-vs-claude-ai-detector-humanizer-2026
- **Why this matters:** The catalog verdict already notes this but the evidence is now overwhelming. Specialized writing tools must differentiate on workflow, brand voice, SEO optimization, or governance — pure generation is a losing game.

### 5. NEW CONTENDER ALERT: WritingTools (theJayTea/WritingTools)

- **~2.1K GitHub stars** (growing fast — was top 10 trending on GitHub Oct 2024)
- System-wide grammar assistant for Windows, Linux, macOS
- Uses Gemini API (free), local LLMs, or any OpenAI-compatible API
- Created by a high school student from Bangalore
- Featured in 28+ global publications (Beebom, XDA, How-To Geek, Neowin, Windows Central)
- One hotkey: fix grammar, optimize text, summarize content system-wide
- Open source, free, no subscriptions
- Positions as "better than Apple Intelligence Writing Tools" — uses ~30B parameter models vs Apple's 3B
- **No HN signal** (0 stories found)
- Source: https://github.com/theJayTea/WritingTools
- **Why this matters:** Fills the "system-wide AI writing assistant" niche that Grammarly occupies but with open-source, LLM-agnostic approach. Not yet at Harper/Vale scale but growth trajectory is strong. Different from Harper (grammar-only, no AI) — WritingTools is AI-powered rewriting + grammar.
